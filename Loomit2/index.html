About Mount St. Helens

Mount St. Helens is most notorious for its major 1980 eruption, the deadliest and most economically destructive volcanic event in the history of the United States.

The volcano is still considered active, evidenced by steam that rises from the ever-growing caldera where a summit used to be.

Recreation

Camping & Backpacking

There is nothing quite like spending an evening away from home oon the slopes of Mount St. Helens. Whether you seek the solitude of a backcountry camping site or a convenient place for you and your family to pitch a tent or park an RV, there are plenty of places to camp.

Explore Camping and Backpacking sites

Climbing

Mount St. Helens provides technical as well as non-technical climbing opportunities. Climbing Mount St. Helens can be extremely dangerous. Climbers should review all safety information and climbing regulations as well as prepare appropriately for the climb. Permits are required year round to climb over 4800 ft.

Learn about Climbing Mount St. Helens

Hiking

There are almost 200 miles of trails open to hiking. Many of these trails travel through designated wilderness areas in the forest. Help us protect fish, wildlife, and your natural resources by practicing a backcountry ethic of leave no trace!

Find a day hike to explore

Winter Sports

There are many options to enjoy the area in the Winter. There are hundreds of miles of backcountry skiing along the slopes of the mountain, cross country skiing through the forests, snowshoeing along with the skiers, and sledding for the kids.

Find a Sno-Park to play in

Spelunking

Mount St. Helens is home to the Ape Cave Lava Tube which is a popular attraction in the Mount St. Helens National Monument and the longest lava tube in the continental United States at over two miles in length. The forest has dozens of lava tubes to explore such as Lake Cave and Ole's Cave, however due to the danger, the Ape Cave is the best to explore.

Learn about the many caves in the area

Trail Running

There are several trail races through the Mount St. Helens National Monument. These courses showcase some of the most stunning views of the area including the desolation zone of Mt. St. Helens, hanging lakes, lava fields, long mountaintop ridge lines with stunning forest, mountain and lake views.

Learn about the areas many trail races
