About Mount St. Helens

Mount St. Helens is most notorious for its major 1980 eruption, the deadliest and most economically destructive volcanic event in the history of the United States.

The volcano is still considered active, evidenced by steam that rises from the ever-growing caldera where a summit used to be.

No matter how long it takes you to reach the crater, the 360-degree view at the summit is well worth the effort.

About the Climb

Mount St. Helens is a popular climb for both beginning and experience mountaineers. With the ability to climb Mount St. Helens year-round, late spring through early fall is the most popular season.

Most climbers use the Monitor Ridge Route from Climbers Bivouac. This route gains 4,500 feet in five miles to the crater rim at 8,365 feet elevation. The non-technical climb is suitable for those in good physical condition which requires scrambling on steep and rugged terrain.

A climbing permit is required year round for all persons traveling above 4,800 feet, however entry into the crater is strictly prohibited.

Recreation

Camping & Cabins

There is nothing quite like spending an evening away from home in the Gifford Pinchot National Forest. Whether you seek the solitude of a backcountry camping site or a convenient place for you and your family to pitch a tent or park an RV, there are plenty of places to camp.

Climbing

Both Mount St. Helens and Mount Adams provide technical as well as non-technical climbing opportunities. Both climbs can be extremely dangerous. Climbers should review all safety information and climbing regulations as well as prepare appropriately for the climb. Each mountain has its own climbing permit system.

Hiking & Backpacking

There are almost 1,500 miles of trails on the forest open to hiking. Many of these trails travel through designated wilderness areas in the forest. Help us protect fish, wildlife, and your natural resources by practicing a backcountry ethic of leave no trace!

Water Activities

There are numerous motorized, non-motorized, and swimming options throughout Gifford Pinchot National Forest. From back country lakes to the reservoirs, there is something for all water sport enthusiasts. Mosquitos can be a problem early in the season.

Winter Sports

There are many options to enjoy the area in the Winter. There are hundreds of miles of snowmobile routes, backcountry skiing along the slopes of the mountain, cross country skiing through the forests, snowshoeing along with the skiers, and sledding along the slopes.

Fishing

The Gifford Pinchot National Forest has more than 20 species of fish in 1,360 miles of streams and over 100 lakes. Three species of anadromous fish (chinook and coho salmon, and steelhead trout) and several species of resident salmonids (rainbow trout, kokanee salmon, brown trout, and cutthroat trout), including two species of char (bull trout and eastern brook trout), are found within Forest waters.

Mountain Biking

More than 1,000 miles of trails are woven throughout the Gifford Pinchot National Forest, and many are open to mountain bikers. Trail guide pamphlets are available at the District Ranger Stations and the Forest Headquarters. Trail conditions often change. By visiting a Ranger Station on your way out of the Forest, and letting a Forest Service representative know about a change in trail conditions--such as a fallen tree or washout--you could be a tremendous help.

Horse Riding & Camping

There are almost 1,000 miles of trails on the forest open to horse riding. Help reduce invasive species on national forest lands the U.S. Forest Service requires the use of weed-free feed within the Pacific Northwest. This includes the Gifford Pinchot National Forest and Mount St. Helens National Volcanic Monument. Weed-free feed includes commercially processed pellets, steamed and rolled grains, or state certified weed-free feed.

Other Activities

Other activities include exploring the numerous caves in the area, practicing target shooting, some of the best trails for OHV riding and camping, and breathtaking picnicking spots to enjoy.
